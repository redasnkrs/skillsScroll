## Build Summary

- Class split: Ranger 5 → Rogue 5 → Fighter 2
- Combat style: Stealthy ranged archer; spike massive burst on round 1 with Dread Ambusher + Assassinate + Extra Attack + Action Surge
- Race: Elf (Wood Elf subrace)
- Background: Charlatan
- Primary stats: Dexterity (17 starting, pushed to 18 via Athlete), Wisdom (16), Constitution (14)
- Core feats: Athlete (+1 Dex, jump boost) and Sharpshooter
- Must-have spells: Hunter’s Mark, Ensnaring Strike, Enhanced Leap, Misty Step, Pass Without Trace

## Level-by-Level Progression

### Level 1 — Ranger 1 [2:20–5:07]
- Natural Explorer: Beast Tamer → grants Find Familiar (choose Raven for Blind) [3:38–4:56]
- Favored Enemy: Bounty Hunter → Investigation proficiency; Ensnaring Strike save is at Disadvantage (synergy) [5:07–6:06]
- Starting skills: Sleight of Hand, Stealth; choose Athletics & Insight over Nature/Animal Handling [10:04–10:36]

### Level 2 — Ranger 2 [10:40–13:16]
- Fighting Style: Archery (+2 to attack rolls) [13:16–13:26]
- Spells:
- Hunter’s Mark (must pick) [11:10–11:46]
- Ensnaring Strike (picks synergize with Bounty Hunter) [12:14–12:40]
- Enhanced Leap is excellent; you’ll add it at L3 spell slot selection [12:47–13:10]

### Level 3 — Ranger 3 (Gloomstalker) [13:33–16:48]
- Subclass: Gloomstalker [13:45–14:02]
- Features:
- Dread Ambusher: +3 initiative; +3m movement first turn; one extra attack that deals +1d8 on first turn [14:11–15:38]
- Superior Darkvision (80 ft) [15:49–16:00]
- Bonus Action Hide (via Gloomstalker feature) [16:00–16:11]
- Umbral Shroud: invisibility in dim light/darkness (up to 10 turns) [16:18–16:35]
- Disguise Self [16:41–16:48]
- Spell choice: Enhanced Leap (3x jump distance) [16:58–17:14]
- Fog Cloud is viable but can be covered by party casters [17:14–18:04]

### Level 4 — Ranger 4 (Feat #1) [18:19–20:20]
- Pick Feats (two total over the run; order can vary):
- Athlete (+1 Dexterity to reach 18; +50% jump; reduced cost to stand from prone). Recommended to take Athlete first for the immediate +1 Dex and better synergy before Assassin arrives [20:08–21:22].
- Sharpshooter (toggle: -5 to attack, +10 damage). If impatient, you can take it first; optimal power spikes after Assassin at Rogue 3 [19:18–20:03].

### Level 5 — Ranger 5 [21:27–22:26]
- Extra Attack (huge power spike) [21:22–21:45]
- Gloomstalker spell: Misty Step (Bonus Action teleport) [21:45–22:15]
- Level 2 Ranger spell: Pass Without Trace (+10 Stealth for party; Concentration) [22:26–23:04]
- Maintain till long rest; helps prevent noisy companions blowing stealth [22:41–23:04]

### Level 6 — Rogue 1 [23:12–24:11]
- Sneak Attack (use with Advantage or ally in melee; not with Disadvantage) [23:22–24:04]
- Expertise (double proficiency): Stealth and Sleight of Hand [24:16–24:57]

### Level 7 — Rogue 2 [25:05–25:47]
- Cunning Action: Hide, Dash, Disengage as Bonus Action (Hide overlaps with Gloomstalker but Dash/Disengage are strong) [25:09–25:42]

### Level 8 — Rogue 3 (Assassin) [25:56–29:18]
- Assassinate features:
- Initiative: Advantage on attacks vs creatures that haven’t acted yet [26:16–26:51]
- Ambush: Attacks against Surprised targets crit [27:04–27:35]
- Assassin’s Alacrity: regain Action and Bonus Action at combat start (post-ambush) [27:46–28:20]
- Round 1 sequence example (after ambush): free opening shot → Alacrity restores actions → Attack (Advantage for Sneak Attack + Sharpshooter) → Extra Attack → Dread Ambusher extra attack (+1d8) → later Action Surge adds more [28:20–29:18]

### Level 9 — Rogue 4 (Feat #2) [29:24–29:40]
- Take whichever feat you didn’t at Level 4:
- Athlete (if not yet) to hit Dex 18
- Sharpshooter (if not yet)

### Level 10 — Rogue 5 (Uncanny Dodge) [29:42–30:27]
- Uncanny Dodge: toggle-on passive to halve damage from hits (ensure it’s enabled) [29:46–30:22]

### Level 11 — Fighter 1 [30:29–31:17]
- Second Wind (Bonus Action self-heal; modest late-game) [30:39–31:11]
- Fighter Fighting Style: Defense (+1 AC) [31:17–31:36]

### Level 12 — Fighter 2 (Action Surge) [31:42–32:38]
- Action Surge: gain an extra Action; with Extra Attack, that’s two more attacks; with Potion of Speed/Haste it gets even crazier [31:51–32:32]
- Expected round 1 attacks baseline: 4 (ambush shot + action chain with Extra Attack + Dread Ambusher). With Action Surge: +2. With speed effects: higher ceilings (5–8) [32:03–32:20]

## Recommended Items & How to Get Them

All items below are from the video; locations include verified references where available.

### Head: Shadow of Menzoberranzan (**Act 1**, Underdark)  
- Effect: Shrouded in Shadow — Invisibility for 2 turns (Short Rest recharge). Great backup to Umbral Shroud; works outside dim light/darkness.  
- How to get:
- Complete “Defeat the Duergar Intruders” for the Myconid Sovereign in the Underdark; he unlocks the secret room in Myconid Colony (Ebonlake Grotto). Pick it up on the ground near the Pale Corpse (X: 52, Y: -70).  
- Reference: [Shadow of Menzoberranzan - bg3.wiki](bg3.wiki/1n)  
- Additional guide: [Baldur’s Gate 3: How to Get Shadow of Menzoberranzan - Deltia's Gaming](deltiasgaming.com/1m)

### Boots: Disintegrating Night Walkers (**Act 1**, Grymforge)  
- Effects: Misty Step (Short Rest recharge); immunity to Enwebbed/Entangled/Ensnared; no slipping on Grease/Ice.  
- How to get: Dropped/worn by Nere in Grymforge (**Act 1**).  
- Reference: [Disintegrating Night Walkers - bg3.wiki](bg3.wiki/2j)

### Gloves (progression):
- Early **Act 1**: Gloves of Power — +1 Sleight of Hand; hit applies -1d4 penalty to enemy attack rolls/saves (Goblin fight outside the Grove).  
- **Act 1** alternative: Hail of Thorns Gloves — grants Hail of Thorns (buy in Myconid Colony merchant).  
- **Act 3** best: Stalker Gloves — +1 Initiative; Sneak Attack gains +1d4 Force damage.  
- How to get: Buy from Exxvikyap at The Rivington General (**Act 3**, Rivington).  
- Reference: [How to Get Stalker Gloves - Deltia's Gaming](deltiasgaming.com/2f)

### Armor: Penumbral Armour (**Act 2**)  
- Effect: AC 12; +3 Stealth when obscured; excellent aesthetic for assassin theme.  
- How to get: In an abandoned house near the Last Light Inn (**Act 2**).  
- References: [Penumbral Armour | Baldurs Gate 3 Wiki - FextraLife](baldursgate3.wiki.fextralife.com/2c), [Where to Find Penumbral Armor - GameRant](gamerant.com/1m)

### Bows (progression):
- Spellthief (**Act 1**, Druid Grove merchant): regain a level 1 spell slot on crit — great early synergy.  
- Joltshooter (**Act 1**, after rescuing Grand Duke): rare longbow upgrade — excellent midgame.  
- The Dead Shot (**Act 3**, Lower City merchant): very rare longbow; reduces crit threshold by 1 (stacking), Keen Attack doubles proficiency — very strong endgame.  
- Legendary option: “Gontr Mael” (post “Disable the Steel Watch” quest).  
- Effects (as described in video): Guiding Bolt debuff application (advantage for next attack), Celestial Haste (free haste for 5 turns without lethargy), Bolt of Celestial Light (fear + radiant damage over time).  
- Note: The glow may conflict with darkness-based stealth setups.

### Cloaks
- Many good capes exist; none strictly build-defining per the video. Dark Urge-specific capes can be particularly strong but are origin-gated.

## Gameplay Tips & Sequencing

- Pre-combat setup:
- Cast Pass Without Trace to cover party stealth [22:41–23:04]
- Use Enhanced Leap or Misty Step for high ground positioning [12:57–13:10], [21:45–22:15]
- Use Umbral Shroud or Shadow of Menzoberranzan to enter ideal position undetected [16:18–16:35], [33:20–33:55]
- Round 1 burst (typical):
  1) Ambush shot (free pre-combat arrow) [28:04–28:20]
  2) Assassin’s Alacrity returns Action+Bonus Action [27:46–28:20]
  3) Attack with Advantage (Assassinate: Initiative), apply Sneak Attack + toggle Sharpshooter [26:16–26:51], [19:18–20:03]
  4) Extra Attack (Ranger 5) [21:22–21:45]
  5) Dread Ambusher extra attack (+1d8) [14:11–15:38]
  6) Action Surge (+2 more attacks baseline) at Fighter 2 [31:51–32:32]  
  Optional: Potion of Speed/Haste to further increase attacks [32:03–32:20]

- Advantage sources to offset Sharpshooter’s -5:
- High ground, Hide, surprise, blinded targets (Raven familiar), Guiding Bolt debuff, party buffs, etc. [12:41–12:47], [16:00–16:11], [4:47–4:56]
- Concentration management:
- Pass Without Trace vs Hunter’s Mark: swap to Pass Without Trace once Extra Attack online; Hunter’s Mark is great early (auto-transfer on kill without spending slot) [11:40–11:46], [22:26–23:04]
- Defensive toggles:
- Ensure Uncanny Dodge is enabled in Passives panel [29:46–30:22]

## Notes & Alternatives Mentioned
- Strength is kept at 10 (not dumped to 8) due to BG3 shove meta; CHA/INT are dumped with option to cover INT via Warped Headband early [9:01–9:32], [8:13–8:28]
- Fog Cloud is useful for stealth but can be covered by casters; Enhanced Leap preferred for elevation access [16:58–18:04]
- You can take Sharpshooter at Level 4 if desired; optimal pairing with Assassin (Rogue 3) to consistently get Advantage [19:18–21:22]
- Misty Step is so strong that having it across party is recommended (video author plans a dedicated guide) [22:10–22:15]
- Gloves progression: pick early power (Gloves of Power / Hail of Thorns), then swap to Stalker Gloves in **Act 3** for Initiative + Sneak Attack damage [35:31–36:07]
